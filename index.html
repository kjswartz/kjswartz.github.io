Featured Project

I built an intramural hockey league management app, for several different user types. Visitors can check on schedules and see stats for teams. Users can get in contact with team members to see if they need any extra players. Team members can see the team player’s schedule, in order to see who will be able to make which games. Administrators can approve new teams, create/modify the schedule, as well as update stats and scores.

About Me

I joined the Air Force after high school, serving seven years as an Intelligence Analyst. I separated in 2011, and then joined the Identity Intelligence Exploitation cell at Special Operations Command (SOCOM), MacDill AFB, FL. It was here that I fell in love with coding. I was dissatisfied with the constant receptiveness, and time involved with formatting the many reports I would write on a daily basis. I stumbled upon macro short cuts for Microsoft Word, and instantly begin writing several macros in Visual Basic. I was able to reduce the time involved with formatting from upwards of an hour, down to the click of a button. When a friend/coworker shared a link with me for The Iron Yard, I instantly became obsessed with the idea of coding full-time! I recently graduated from The Iron Yard's Ruby on Rails course and I am currently looking to join a team of developers to solidify and expand my current skill-set.

My Works

Below are some of the web apps/projects I built while attending The Iron Yard. I built these apps using Ruby 2.2.0 and Rails 4.2.1. I also utilized Javascript(CoffeeScript), jQuery plugins, Bourbon, SASS, HTML5/CSS3. All logins were setup utilizing bcrypt. I used the carrierwave gem for uploading images, and Amazon Web Services for hosting them. All of these apps are hosted on Heroku.

The first movie returned via JSON, is parsed and displayed.
